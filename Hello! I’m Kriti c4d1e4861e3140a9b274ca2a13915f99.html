What I like about Product?

I’ve had some time to think about this.

  1. Illusion of power - the intersectional nature of product brings with it an illusion of power, wherein you’ll be closely be working with senior stakeholders as well as customers.
  1. Eagle eye view - When you’re managing a product, you get to have an eagle’s eye view on all things related to your product - so you’re the director of the show.
  1. Short Gratification Cycle - This is complimentary to shipping quality product fast. You get to see the real time impact of your product.
  1. Empathy- One of the most important skills needed to be a good PM is empathy in order to truly understand the challenges faced by your users
  1. Accountability - I enjoy high accountability and like being in charge - possibly because I’m the elder sibling.
  1. Good money - The paycheck doesn’t hurt
  1. Unstructured - I am someone who thrives in chaos, so when you’re at the centre of multiple functions, it tends to get chaotic. Creativity comes out unstructured while execution requires structure.
  1. Eye for Detail - I believe being a good PM is about paying great attention to detail of your product - how one feature would affect another and how to connect it seamlessly.
  1. Adaptability - Product allows you the flexibility to adapt very quickly to the changing times since you’re not bound to any tech stack. You work with tools and tools can be adapted quickly.
Cons of Product

These are reflections of my own journey.

  1. Imposter Syndrome
  1. No real control - you can only lead by influence
  1. In large orgs, you have to really fight a battle to get your project prioritised across multiple teams that are already running behind their roadmap.

Some principles that have helped me work better

  1. Raising difficult topics without being difficult to work with
  1. Democratising the right information to the right people
  1. Involving devs for ideation and validation
  1. Ensuring that decisions get made that will drive the product forward
  1. Thing big, act small
  1. Valuing outcomes over output
  1. Make things simpler
